Surry Hills is an inner‑city suburb located approximately 2 km southeast of the Sydney CBD. It is known for its vibrant café culture, creative industries, terrace‑house streetscapes, and strong nightlife scene. The suburb borders Darlinghurst, Redfern, Haymarket, and Moore Park, and is one of Sydney’s most walkable neighbourhoods.

Population and Demographics:
Surry Hills has a population of roughly 16,000 residents. The suburb attracts a high proportion of young professionals, creatives, and hospitality workers. Around 45% of residents were born overseas, with strong representation from the UK, New Zealand, China, and Southeast Asia. The age profile skews young, with a large concentration of residents aged 25–39. The area also has a significant LGBTQ+ community and a high proportion of single‑person households.

Housing and Tenure:
Surry Hills is dominated by Victorian and Federation‑era terrace houses, converted warehouses, boutique apartment blocks, and modern mid‑rise developments. Approximately 70% of dwellings are units or terraces, and rental occupancy is high, with around 55% of residents renting. Owner‑occupiers tend to be long‑term residents or higher‑income professionals. Housing stock varies widely in condition, from renovated designer terraces to older walk‑up apartments.

Rental Market:
Rental prices in Surry Hills are among the highest in the inner‑city area due to its proximity to the CBD and strong lifestyle appeal. One‑bedroom units typically range from $700–$850 per week, while two‑bedroom units range from $900–$1,200. Renovated terraces and townhouses often exceed $1,300 per week. Rental demand is consistently strong, particularly among professionals working in the CBD, tech, media, and creative industries.

Historical Rental Trend:
Over the past decade, Surry Hills has experienced significant rental growth, with median unit rents rising roughly 45–55% since the early 2010s. The suburb saw rapid increases between 2021 and 2024 as inner‑city living regained popularity post‑pandemic.

Property Prices:
Surry Hills is considered a premium inner‑city suburb with strong long‑term capital growth. Older one‑bedroom units typically range from $750k–$950k, while modern apartments can exceed $1.1M. Terrace houses generally range from $1.8M–$2.8M depending on size, renovation quality, and street location. Larger or architect‑designed terraces can exceed $3M.

Historical Price Trend:
Surry Hills has shown strong appreciation over the past 20 years. In the mid‑2000s, one‑bedroom units were commonly priced around $350k–$450k, while terraces ranged from $700k–$1M. By the mid‑2010s, unit prices had risen to $600k–$750k, and terraces often exceeded $1.5M. The 2020s saw further growth driven by demand for walkable neighbourhoods, proximity to tech and creative industries, and limited housing supply.

Transport and Connectivity:
Surry Hills is one of Sydney’s most accessible suburbs. The CBD is within walking distance, and the suburb is served by the Light Rail (Surry Hills and Moore Park stops), multiple bus routes, and nearby Central Station. Cycling infrastructure is well developed, and the suburb has easy access to major roads including Cleveland Street and South Dowling Street.

Lifestyle and Amenities:
Surry Hills is known for its café culture, boutique shops, small bars, and multicultural dining options. Crown Street is the main commercial strip, offering restaurants, bakeries, and specialty stores. The suburb has a strong creative presence, with galleries, studios, and design agencies. Moore Park and Prince Alfred Park provide green spaces, sports facilities, and outdoor recreation.

Safety and Community:
Surry Hills is generally safe, though nightlife areas can be busy during late hours. The suburb has a strong community identity, with active local groups and a diverse, inclusive population.

Schools and Healthcare:
Local schools include Surry Hills Public School and Sydney Boys and Sydney Girls High Schools in nearby Moore Park. The suburb is close to major healthcare facilities including St Vincent’s Hospital in Darlinghurst and the medical precinct around Central Station.

Overall Assessment:
Surry Hills is a highly desirable inner‑city suburb offering exceptional walkability, strong lifestyle appeal, and proximity to the CBD. It is well suited for young professionals, creatives, and long‑term residents seeking a vibrant urban environment with stable property values and strong rental demand.
