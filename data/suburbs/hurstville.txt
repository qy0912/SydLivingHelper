Hurstville is a major commercial and residential hub in the St George region, located approximately 16 km south of the Sydney CBD. It is known for its large Chinese community, extensive retail precinct, and strong transport connections. The suburb borders Kogarah, Beverly Hills, Allawah, and Penshurst.

Population and Demographics:
Hurstville has a population of around 30,000 residents. The suburb has one of the highest concentrations of Chinese‑Australian residents in Sydney, with Mandarin and Cantonese widely spoken. Around 70% of residents were born overseas, and more than 75% speak a language other than English at home. The age distribution includes a mix of young families, students, and older long‑term residents.

Housing and Tenure:
Hurstville is dominated by high‑density apartment buildings, particularly around the station and Forest Road. Approximately 80% of dwellings are units, with a mix of older brick apartments from the 1970s–1990s and newer high‑rise developments built after 2010. Around 55% of residents rent, reflecting the suburb’s appeal to students, new migrants, and young professionals.

Rental Market:
Rental prices in Hurstville are moderate relative to its amenities. One‑bedroom units typically range from $520–$620 per week, while two‑bedroom units range from $650–$820. Newer high‑rise apartments command higher rents. Rental demand is consistently strong due to proximity to shops, schools, and transport.

Historical Rental Trend:
Over the past decade, Hurstville’s rental prices have increased by roughly 35–45%. Growth accelerated between 2021 and 2024 as demand for high‑amenity suburbs increased and new migrants returned to Sydney.

Property Prices:
Hurstville offers a wide range of unit prices. Older two‑bedroom units typically range from $600k–$750k, while newer apartments can exceed $850k. Free‑standing houses are limited and generally priced above $1.8M due to strong demand and limited supply.

Historical Price Trend:
In the mid‑2000s, typical two‑bedroom units were priced around $350k–$450k. By the mid‑2010s, prices had risen to $550k–$700k. The 2020s saw further appreciation driven by population growth, infrastructure upgrades, and strong demand from Chinese‑Australian families.

Transport and Connectivity:
Hurstville Station is a major interchange on the T4 Illawarra Line, offering frequent services to the CBD, Wolli Creek, and Cronulla. Travel time to the city is around 22 minutes. Multiple bus routes connect the suburb to surrounding areas. The suburb also has easy access to King Georges Road and the M5.

Lifestyle and Amenities:
Hurstville is a major retail centre, anchored by Westfield Hurstville, which includes supermarkets, department stores, cinemas, and a wide range of dining options. The suburb has numerous Asian restaurants, grocery stores, and specialty shops. Parks and recreational facilities include Hurstville Oval and local community centres.

Safety and Community:
Hurstville is generally safe, though the station area can be busy during peak hours. The suburb has a strong community presence, with many cultural events and family‑oriented activities.

Schools and Healthcare:
Local schools include Hurstville Public School, Hurstville South Public School, and Danebank Anglican School for Girls. The suburb is close to St George Hospital in Kogarah.

Overall Assessment:
Hurstville is a highly convenient suburb with strong transport links, extensive retail options, and a vibrant multicultural community. It is well suited for families, students, and new migrants seeking amenities and accessibility.
